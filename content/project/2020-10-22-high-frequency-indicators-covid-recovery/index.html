Open Table

According to Open Table data of seated diners at restaurants, CA is -91% below where it was last year. Overall, the United States is -60% below baseline.

The graph below shows the 7-day moving average of seated covers.

Apple Mobility Data

Apple mobility data is an index based on the number of people seeking directions through the Apple Maps app (driving, transit, walking). This is a 7-day moving average of demand for directions.

As of December 10 the 7-day moving average for:

  • Driving directions was 105
  • Walking directions was 106
  • Transit directions was 46

The baseline comparison index is set to 100 and is based on the week of January 13, 2020.

Time Spent Away from Home

Google has been tracking the GPS locations of citizens to get a sense of how frequently people are visiting certain locations. Below is a plot a 7-day average of how frequently people have been away of their homes. The data is indexed to Jan 3 - Feb 6, 2020.

The 7-day average for time Americans spend away from home is -11.6% below the baseline index.

Box Office Data

Obviously, we know that there isn’t much interest in going to the movies, and studios have limited their slate of movies, but we can think of the Box Office data as a heuristic for when people will feel comfortable going into indoor spaces again, post-covid.

For the week of Dec 4-10 the U.S. Box Office total was $10,249,961

Total US Spending

U.S. spending data comes in at a bit of a lag, so the latest day we have data for is November 29. Spending is -12% below normal.

Spending by Income Level

Approximately 70% of US GDP is driven by consumer spending, and a lot of that consumer spending is normally driven by households in the highest income brackets. But we are seeing strong splits in terms of spending by income levels – as of November 29 spending by the highest income zip codes is still -14% below where it was prior to the pandemic.
