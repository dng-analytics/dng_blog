An article summarizing some of the changes and upgrades required of dealerships as the EV revolution grows near.

The initial EV investment isn’t all dealers have to weigh. Electric vehicles require less maintenance, which means fewer trips to the dealership’s service department. And, as manufacturers contend, longer-lasting vehicles won’t need to be replaced as often as a gas-powered vehicles produced on traditional assembly lines. “There’s significantly less opportunity for revenue after the sale,” Laethem said, "so that is going to be probably the biggest disruption.

EV’s only make up about 2% of total sales, and there are regional disparities in terms of consumer interest. As OEMs have pushed dealers to upgrade, some have balked. At least 150 GM dealers decided to part ways with Cadillac at the end of 2020.
