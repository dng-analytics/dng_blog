From QSR Magazine a pretty comprehensive overview of consumer demand for chicken sandwiches.

Some of the findings:

Look at the gains Popeye’s earned with its chicken sandwich (and how their gains came at the expense of all other brands), but note that Chick-fil-a is still pulling in 45% of the overall market share over the duration.

And from RMS, a survey in the first half of February on brand awareness and cravings of chicken sandwiches. Chick-fil-a wins on customer awareness and craving despite having a limited footprint. But honestly, look at the room to play in the craving category. Only 30% crave Chick-fil-a despite 75% awareness.

Some other results reported here:

More than 70 percent of respondents said they believe a chicken sandwich should be priced lower than a beef burger

Only 51 percent said they buy chicken as a more affordable option

46 percent believe chicken is better value than beef

As QSR notes, this should highlight the price sensitivity that customers have with chicken sandwiches. They don’t find them nearly as indulgent as beef.
